# Brainstorming Skill

This skill MUST be used before any creative work, especially when creating new features, building components, adding functionality, or modifying behavior. Its goal is to explore user intent, requirements, and design before implementation begins.

## When to use
- At the start of a task involving creative decisions.
- When requirements are vague or high-level.
- When proposing a new UI component or visual design.
- Before refactoring major parts of the codebase.

## Goals
- Understand the "why" behind the user's request.
- Define the core user experience and flow.
- Identify potential constraints or edge cases early.
- Align on a visual and technical approach.

## Steps
1. **Clarify Intent**: Ask the user about their goals and who the feature is for.
2. **Explore Requirements**: Identify mandatory vs. optional features.
3. **Design Discussion**: Propose and iterate on visual and structural ideas.
4. **Feasibility Check**: Briefly discuss the technical approach and potential risks.
5. **Output**: Summarize the agreed-upon design and requirements before proceeding to planning.
